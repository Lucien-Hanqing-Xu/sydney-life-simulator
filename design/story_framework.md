# Sydney Life Simulator — Story Framework v1

## Game Overview

**Core concept**: You choose one of four characters with different backgrounds, visa types, and starting conditions, and experience life in Sydney through their eyes. Same city, completely different lives.

**Game flow**: Character select → Ch1 → Ch2 → Ch3 → Ch4 → Ch5 (ending). Linear progression, but choices change stats. Later choices may be gated by stats. Ending determined by final stat values.

**Stat model** (each 0–10):
- **money** (💰): Economic security
- **visa** (🛂): Visa stability / immigration status security
- **skill** (🔧): Career capital, professional ability, language
- **mood** (💭): Mental health, happiness, sense of purpose

## Character 1: 小敏 (Fern) — WHV · 底层东亚女性

**Background**: 25 years old, from a small town in China. Grew up in a struggling family — parents divorced, raised by mom who works in a factory. Came to Australia on a Working Holiday Visa to earn money and see the world. Her mom still calls every week asking her to send money back. She arrived with one suitcase and 2000 AUD.

**Starting stats**: 💰2 🛂2 🔧2 💭6

**Core arc**: From nothing to something. The question: can she break free from the gravity of her origin?

---

### Ch1: 落地悉尼 (Landing in Sydney)

**Setting**: Sydney Airport → Ashfield share house

**Narrative**: 小敏 lands at Sydney airport, overwhelmed by the English announcements she can barely understand. She takes a bus to Ashfield, finds her share house — a cramped room with 3 other WHV girls. Roommate 小雯 has been here 3 months, already a veteran. Over instant noodles that night, 小雯 gives her the survival briefing: "先注册 Uber Eats 和 DoorDash，新人有奖励加成，头几周能多赚不少。买个电瓶车就能开工。等新人奖励结束了再想下一步。" That night, lying on her thin mattress, her mom calls: "到了吗？那边工资高吧？什么时候能寄钱回来？"

**Choices**:
- **A "先安顿好再说"** → Tell mom you need time to settle first. Honest but sets a boundary.
  - Effect: 💭+1
- **B "放心妈，我尽快寄钱"** → Promise to send money soon. Familiar role: the dutiful daughter.
  - Effect: 💭-1 (pressure)
- **C "挂掉电话，看着窗外的夜"** → Don't answer properly, just stare out the window at the unfamiliar city.
  - Effect: (no change, avoidance)

**Image note**: Airport arrival hall → cramped share house room at night, two girls eating instant noodles, phone screen glowing.

---

### Ch2: 电瓶车 (The E-bike)

**Setting**: Ashfield streets → Sydney suburbs at night

**Narrative**: 小敏 follows 小雯's advice — registers for Uber Eats, gets an ABN. But first she needs an e-bike. New ones cost $2000+, way beyond her budget. She scrolls 小红书 and finds a post: "急出 九成新电瓶车 续航10小时 大电池 刚骑三个月 $650 自取Burwood". She goes to see it — a Chinese guy demonstrates it briefly around the block, battery shows full. She hands over $650, almost a third of her savings.

First two weeks are golden. Uber Eats new rider bonus kicks in — orders come non-stop, every delivery has extra incentive pay. She works 8-10 hour shifts, lunch rush then dinner rush, and clears $1200+ a week. She starts to feel like maybe 小雯 was right. She sends $500 home. Mom is happy for the first time.

Then on a long Friday evening shift, 4.5 hours in — the battery indicator drops from 25% to dead without warning. The motor cuts out. She's somewhere past Strathfield, carrying a bag of cold pad thai, on a street she doesn't recognize. The app is pinging: "Delivery delayed — customer waiting." She pushes the dead bike in the dark, sweat and tears mixing. Her phone is at 15%.

A porch light flicks on. An older woman — an Australian, maybe in her 50s — comes out in a bathrobe. "Are you alright, love?" 小敏's English fails her, but the woman understands. She wheels the bike into the garage, plugs it in, makes 小敏 a cup of tea. They sit on the porch waiting for enough charge. The woman doesn't ask about visas. She just says: "Sydney can be tough when you're new. But you'll be alright." 小敏 doesn't understand every word. She understands enough.

The e-bike's real battery life: about 4-5 hours. Half of the seller's claimed 10 hours. An 8-hour delivery shift is impossible on one charge. The $650 she can't get back.

**Choices** (how she responds to the setback):
- **A "找卖家算账"** → Go back to Xiaohongshu, confront the seller. Demand a partial refund. He blocks her. She posts a detailed warning for others — first time she speaks up publicly.
  - Effect: 💭+1 (assertiveness, learned to fight back), 💰+0 (no refund)
- **B "认了，自己想办法"** → Accept the loss. Buy a second battery ($200), swap mid-shift at home, plan routes around charging breaks. Adapt and grind.
  - Effect: 💰-1, 🔧+1 (resourcefulness), 💭+0
- **C "接受现实，开始想下一步"** → The scam is a wake-up call. Delivery isn't long-term. She starts researching White Card and RSA — real tickets to better work.
  - Effect: 🔧+1, 💭+1 (turning pain into clarity)

**Image note**: Xiaohongshu listing for the e-bike / 小敏 pushing dead bike on dark suburban street / kind woman on porch with tea / 小敏 looking at her phone, researching courses.

---

### Ch3: 转型 (Leveling Up)

**Setting**: 2-3 months in. Uber Eats new rider bonus has ended. Delivery income drops by a third. Time to evolve.

**Narrative**: The new rider bonus is over. Where she used to get back-to-back orders with bonus pay, now there are dead hours where the app is silent and she's circling blocks burning battery for nothing. The math doesn't work anymore — not with a 4.5-hour battery, not with winter coming, not with the platform taking a bigger cut every month. 小雯 has already pivoted: she got her RSA and works at a bar in the CBD now. "送餐就是过渡，别当长期饭碗。" 小敏 realizes she needs to level up. Two paths: the White Card (construction, physical, $30+/hr cash) or the RSA (hospitality, service, steadier hours).

**Choices**:
- **A "考白卡，去工地"** → Pay $80 for the White Card course (one day). Get into construction — demolition cleanup, site laboring. Hard physical work, but cash-in-hand and $30-35/hr. She's the only woman on most sites.
  - Effect: 💰+2, 🔧+1, 💭-1 (tough, isolating)
- **B "考RSA，进餐馆或超市"** → Get the RSA certificate. Find work at a restaurant or supermarket. Steadier hours, more social, practices English. Less money than construction.
  - Effect: 💰+1, 🔧+2 (English + customer service skills), 💭+1
- **C "两个都考，看哪个先来活"** → Hedge. Get both certifications. Takes more time and money upfront but maximizes options.
  - Requirement: 💰 >= 4
  - Effect: 💰-1, 🔧+2, 💭+0
- **D "还是继续送餐吧，至少自由"** → Stick with delivery. She knows the game, doesn't want to start over again. But income keeps sliding.
  - Effect: 💰+0, 🔧+0, 💭-2 (stagnation + frustration)

**Image note**: Construction site with hard hat / supermarket or restaurant kitchen / 小敏 studying for White Card test at share house desk / rainy night delivery, empty streets.

---

### Ch4: 十字路口 (Crossroads)

**Setting**: 6-8 months in. She's worked delivery, then construction/hospitality. WHV clock ticking. The big question: what's next?

**Narrative**: 小敏 has been in Australia long enough to understand how it works. She's heard the stories a hundred times in the share house, in the WeChat groups, at work: 二签需要去偏远地区做满88天指定工作。有人去了昆士兰摘草莓，有人去了南澳肉厂，有人去了塔州农场。Those who came back had stories of loneliness, backbreaking work, and sunburn — but also a second year, and the beginning of a path. Meanwhile, she's saved some money. Maybe enough. Maybe not.

She looks at her bank account. She looks at the WHV expiry date. She looks at Carsales: a 2009 Toyota RAV4, 180,000km, $6,500.

**Choices**:
- **A "买车，去偏远地区集签"** → Buy an old Japanese SUV. Pack her things. Drive north/south to regional Australia for the 88 days of specified work. The road to a second-year WHV, and maybe — eventually — something more permanent.
  - Requirement: 💰 >= 6
  - Effect: 💰-4, 🛂+3, 🔧+1, 💭+1 (hope, agency)
- **B "不想走了，在悉尼继续熬"** → Stay in Sydney. Keep working. Maybe something will come up. Maybe it won't. She tells herself "再看看吧" for the third time this month.
  - Effect: 💰+1, 🛂+0, 💭-1 (drift)
- **C "算了，回家吧"** → Book the one-way ticket. She's tired. The scam, the construction dust, the cold delivery nights, mom's voice on the phone getting older — she's done.
  - Effect: 🛂→0, 💭 varies (relief? defeat? both?)
- **D "找个男朋友解决身份问题"** → Someone suggests "找个local谈恋爱拿PR." She's heard this advice from too many people.
  - Effect: 🛂+1, 💭-2 (trading autonomy for security — she knows it, and it eats at her)

**Image note**: Old RAV4 on Carsales listing / 小敏 loading bags into the car trunk / highway stretching into flat regional landscape / airport departure, looking back one last time.

---

### Ch5: Endings

Based on final stats after Ch4 — **three endings**:

**Ending A — "踏上远路" (The Road Ahead)**
- Condition: 🛂 >= 5 AND 💰 >= 3 AND 💭 >= 4
- Story: 小敏 buys a beat-up 2009 RAV4 with 180,000km on the clock. She packs everything she owns into the back — it doesn't fill half the trunk. The share house girls help her load up. 小雯 gives her a portable phone charger and a hug: "到了给我发消息。"

  She drives out of Sydney on the M1, heading north. The city thins out — Hornsby, then Hawkesbury, then nothing but road and sky. She's never driven this far in her life. The GPS says 8 hours to the strawberry farm. The RAV4 rattles past 100km/h. The radio plays songs she doesn't know.

  She doesn't know if she'll get the 88 days. She doesn't know if the second-year visa will lead to anything permanent. She doesn't even know if the car will make it. But for the first time since she landed in Sydney, she's driving toward something instead of running from something. The road is long. So is she.

**Ending B — "黯然回国" (Quiet Departure)**
- Condition: 💭 < 3 OR (🛂 < 3 AND 💰 < 3)
- Story: She books the cheapest one-way ticket home. At the airport, she sits in the terminal with her one suitcase — the same one she arrived with, a little more battered now. She scrolls through photos on her phone: the share house, the e-bike, a blurry selfie at the construction site, the porch in Strathfield.

  On the plane, she stares out the window as Sydney shrinks below. The harbour, the bridge, the suburban grids where she pushed a dead bike in the dark. She doesn't cry. She thought she would, but she doesn't. She's just... empty.

  Back home, mom is at the arrivals gate. She looks older. She doesn't say "你怎么回来了." She just holds her daughter. Sometimes things don't work out. That's not failure. But right now, it feels like it.

**Ending C — "继续迷茫" (Still Drifting)**
- Condition: (none of the above — default/middle state)
- Story: She stays in Sydney. The WHV has months left, or maybe she extended somehow. She works, she eats, she sleeps. The share house changes roommates; 小雯 moved out, new girls move in with the same questions she once asked. She gives them the same briefing 小雯 gave her: "办ABN，注册Uber Eats，买个电瓶车..."

  Some nights she opens Carsales and looks at RAV4s. Some nights she opens Skyscanner and looks at flights home. She closes both apps and opens Uber Eats instead. Another order. Another delivery. The battery is at 60%. Enough for now.

## Character 2: 思琪 (Chelsea) — Student Visa · 上海白富美

**Background**: 22 years old, from a wealthy Shanghai family. Dad runs a real estate development company. Mom is a former model. 思琪 grew up in the spotlight — pretty, well-off, surrounded by people. She chose to study Media & Communications / Commerce at USYD (传媒+商科双学位, the classic rich-kid combo). Dad bought her an apartment in Darling Harbour before she even landed. Back in Shanghai she had admirers, followers, everything. She came to Sydney expecting more of the same.

**Starting stats**: 💰8 🛂5 🔧3 💭6

**Core arc**: She has beauty and money. Then someone weaponizes both against her. The question: after the world tries to define her by rumors, can she define herself?

---

### Ch1: 悉尼名媛 (Sydney It-Girl)

**Setting**: Darling Harbour apartment → USYD → Sydney's finest restaurants and galleries

**Narrative**: 思琪's Sydney life looks like a curated Instagram feed. She lives in a two-bedroom apartment overlooking Darling Harbour — floor-to-ceiling windows, marble kitchen she never cooks in. Her days: brunch at Bills in Surry Hills, lectures at USYD (she attends most of them, vaguely), shopping in Westfield Sydney, dinner at fancy Japanese or Italian restaurants, weekends at the Art Gallery of NSW or White Rabbit Gallery. She posts everything. Her Xiaohongshu has 20k followers. Her WeChat Moments get 200+ likes.

In the Chinese student circle, she's the girl everyone knows about. The guys orbit her — buying her coffee, offering to drive her places. The girls are friendly to her face. She's not naive enough to think it's all genuine, but she's comfortable. This is the world she knows.

**Choices**:
- **A "享受这一切，你值得"** → Lean into the lifestyle. She's young, rich, and in Sydney. Why not? More restaurants, more galleries, more followers.
  - Effect: 💭+1, 🔧+0 (comfort, no growth)
- **B "也认真搞搞学业吧"** → Start actually engaging with coursework. A media professor assigns a project on brand storytelling — it catches her interest more than she expected.
  - Effect: 💭+0, 🔧+2 (discovery of genuine interest)
- **C "交一些留学生圈以外的朋友"** → Venture beyond the Chinese student bubble. Meet locals, other internationals. It's awkward but refreshing — people who don't know her family, don't care about her bag.
  - Effect: 💭+0, 🔧+1 (broader perspective, support network for later)

**Image note**: Darling Harbour apartment view at sunset / brunch flat-lay / Art Gallery of NSW interior / 思琪 taking a selfie in front of a painting, surrounded but alone.

---

### Ch2: 黄谣 (The Rumors)

**Setting**: USYD campus → WeChat groups → 思琪's apartment

**Narrative**: It starts with a screenshot in a group chat she's not in. A guy she rejected — 张浩, commerce student, family money from Shenzhen, ego the size of the Harbour Bridge — starts telling people they hooked up. Then details get added, fabricated, grotesque. Another girl who's always been jealous starts "confirming" the stories, adding her own embellishments. Within a week, it's in every Chinese student WeChat group. Strangers DM her with the screenshots. Someone made a fake account posting manipulated photos.

思琪 is blindsided. She's been talked about before — rich girls always are — but this is different. This is violent. She can't eat. She can't go to campus without feeling eyes on her. She tries to clarify in the group chats — "这些都是假的" — but retractions don't travel as far as rumors. People she thought were friends go quiet. The comments under her Xiaohongshu posts turn vile.

**Choices**:
- **A "忍了，清者自清"** → Try to ride it out. Delete the apps. Stop going out. Wait for people to forget.
  - Effect: 💭-3, 🔧+0 (the pain festers; the rumors don't stop, they just become "old news" she still lives with)
- **B "拿起法律武器"** → Lawyer up. She has the money and she's done being a victim. Find a defamation lawyer. Collect evidence — screenshots, timestamps, witnesses. Send legal letters. File complaints with the university.
  - Effect: 💰-2, 💭-1 (painful process, but empowering), 🔧+2 (learns to fight, understands systems)
- **C "让爸妈来处理"** → Call home. Dad's connections, family's resources. Let them make it go away.
  - Effect: 💭-1, 💰+0, 🔧-1 (problem solved but she didn't solve it; reinforces dependence)

**Image note**: Phone screen showing vicious WeChat messages / 思琪 alone in her luxury apartment, curtains drawn / lawyer's office, she's signing documents / campus hallway, whispers everywhere.

---

### Ch3: 散心 (Healing)

**Setting**: Beyond Sydney — Australia's landscapes

**Narrative**: The legal process plays out (or doesn't, depending on her choice). Either way, the semester from hell is over. 思琪 is exhausted — not the cute kind of tired she used to post about, but the deep bone-weariness of having your dignity publicly shredded and fighting to get it back. She doesn't want to see another group chat, another campus corridor, another Darling Harbour sunset that reminds her of the apartment where she cried for a week.

So she flies. Melbourne for coffee and street art. Twelve Apostles at dawn. Tasmania's Cradle Mountain in the mist. Uluru at sunset. Whitsundays, where the water is so blue it doesn't look real. She goes alone. She stops posting. For the first time, she experiences Australia beyond the Sydney bubble — and it's vast and quiet and doesn't know her name.

Somewhere in this process — maybe on a hiking trail, maybe in a small-town café — she starts noticing things with her commercial brain switched on. A gap in the market. An idea forming.

**Choices**:
- **A "用旅行治愈自己"** → Fully immerse. Slow travel, journaling, no social media. Let the landscapes do the work.
  - Effect: 💭+3, 💰-2 (expensive but transformative)
- **B "旅行途中，开始观察商机"** → She can't turn off her brain. She notices: Chinese tourists struggle with X, this regional product has no online presence, the wellness tourism market is underserved... She starts a notes file on her phone.
  - Effect: 💭+2, 🔧+2, 💰-1 (healing + clarity)
- **C "旅行只是逃避，该回去面对了"** → Cut the trip short. She knows running isn't healing. Go back to Sydney, face campus, re-engage.
  - Requirement: 💭 >= 4 (needs minimum resilience to choose this)
  - Effect: 💭+1, 🔧+1 (courage), 💰+0

**Image note**: Twelve Apostles at dawn, 思琪 tiny against the cliffs / Cradle Mountain mist / Whitsundays aerial / her alone in a small-town café with a notebook, hair in a messy bun, no makeup — a version of her we haven't seen before.

---

### Ch4: 毕业前 (Before Graduation)

**Setting**: Back in Sydney. Final semester. The degree is almost done.

**Narrative**: 思琪 comes back to Sydney different. The apartment in Darling Harbour is the same, but she isn't. She finishes her degree — average grades, nothing spectacular, but she got through it. The rumors have faded to background noise. Some people still whisper; she's learned not to hear them. 张浩 transferred to Melbourne after the university investigation. Small victory.

Now: the student visa has an expiry date. Dad calls: "回来吧，你读完了，这边给你安排了几个相亲对象，条件都很好。" Mom sends photos of suitable young men from suitable families. The Shanghai machine is waiting to absorb her back.

But she also has that notes file on her phone. Pages and pages of business ideas from the road trip. One idea in particular keeps nagging her — maybe a brand, maybe a platform, something that connects her media skills with the gap she spotted in the market.

**Choices**:
- **A "留下来，把那个想法做出来"** → Apply for the 485 post-study work visa. Use her savings to bootstrap the business idea. It's terrifying — no safety net of Dad's money (or at least, choosing not to use it).
  - Requirement: 🔧 >= 5
  - Effect: 🛂+2, 🔧+2, 💭+2 (bet on herself)
- **B "回上海，接受安排"** → Go home. The fight is over. She's tired. Maybe an arranged marriage isn't the worst thing. At least it's easy. At least she won't be alone.
  - Effect: 🛂→0, 💰+2, 💭-2 (surrender)
- **C "先留着，再看看"** → Get the 485 visa, but don't commit to anything. Enjoy Sydney. Maybe travel more. Figure it out later. There's always later.
  - Effect: 🛂+1, 💭+0, 🔧+0 (comfortable stasis)
- **D "被人骗了"** → She trusts a business partner (or a romantic interest) who turns out to be using her — for money, for connections. The betrayal echoes the rumors: once again, someone used her. This time she trusted them.
  - Requirement: 💭 <= 4 (vulnerable state = susceptible)
  - Effect: 💰-3, 💭-3 (devastation)

**Image note**: Dad on video call with photos of 相亲对象 / 思琪 at a desk with business plans / Shanghai skyline through airplane window / a man's hand on her shoulder, she looks uncertain.

---

### Ch5: Endings

Based on final stats after Ch4 — **three endings**:

**Ending A — "她的名字" (Her Own Name)**
- Condition: 🔧 >= 6 AND 💭 >= 5
- Story: 思琪 registers her company with ASIC on a Tuesday afternoon. It's just her, a laptop, and an ABN — the same three-letter acronym 小敏 used for Uber Eats, but 思琪 doesn't know that. They live in the same city and have never met.

  The brand starts small. She runs it from the Darling Harbour apartment, does her own social media (ironic — the girl who was destroyed by social media now builds a business on it). The first month she makes $400. The second month, $1,200. It's not her father's money. It's hers.

  One evening she's editing product photos at her kitchen counter and catches her reflection in the dark window — no makeup, hair tied back, the harbour lights behind her. She looks like nobody's daughter and nobody's rumor. She looks like herself. It took Sydney, and the worst year of her life, to meet this person.

**Ending B — "回笼" (The Gilded Cage)**
- Condition: 💭 < 3 OR chose to go back in Ch4
- Story: She flies back to Shanghai. First class — Dad upgraded her ticket. The apartment is ready. Mom has arranged dinner with a "很优秀的男生, 家里做金融的." He opens the car door for her. He's polite. He's suitable. He doesn't ask about Australia.

  At the dinner table, surrounded by crystal and laughter, 思琪 smiles on cue. She's good at this. She was always good at this. The Darling Harbour apartment will be rented out. The business idea stays in her phone's notes app, between a screenshot of a Cradle Mountain sunset and a photo she never posted.

  It's not a bad life. It's just not hers.

**Ending C — "悬浮" (Suspended)**
- Condition: (default / middle state)
- Story: 思琪 stays in Sydney on the 485 visa. She still lives in the Darling Harbour apartment. She still goes to nice restaurants, but less often now, and she actually tastes the food. She opens the notes app sometimes and scrolls through the business ideas. Some days they feel possible. Most days they feel like someone else's dream.

  She's not unhappy. She's not happy either. She's suspended — between Shanghai and Sydney, between her father's world and her own, between the girl she was and the woman she almost became. The visa has 18 months left. That feels like enough time. It also feels like nothing.

## Character 3: Linh / Ruth (灵) — Spouse Visa · 越南中年女性

**Background**: 38 years old, from Ho Chi Minh City. Her husband Minh is doing a PhD in Civil Engineering at UNSW. She came on a dependent/spouse visa (subclass 309). Back in Vietnam she ran a small pho stall; here she dreams of a proper restaurant. Her English is functional but accented. She is warm, practical, and tougher than she looks. She has a 10-year-old daughter, Mei, who is adjusting to Australian primary school.

**Starting stats**: 💰4 🛂5 🔧5 💭5

**Special rule**: Linh's 🛂 visa stability is secretly tied to her marriage. Certain choices that strain the marriage add a hidden "marriage_tension" counter. If marriage_tension >= 3, her visa becomes at risk in Ch4.

**Core arc**: She builds something of her own, but her visa depends on someone else. The question: what happens when independence and security pull in opposite directions?

---

### Ch1: 异乡的厨房 (A Kitchen Far from Home)

**Setting**: Kensington apartment → Marrickville shopfront

**Narrative**: Linh has been in Sydney for 6 months. Minh leaves for the lab at 7am and comes back at 10pm. Mei is at school. Linh has found a small empty shopfront on Marrickville Road — the rent is steep but the location is perfect (Vietnamese community nearby, foot traffic). She can taste the pho in her imagination. But the savings are limited.

**Choices**:
- **A "全部身家投进去，开店"** → Go all-in on the restaurant. It's a risk but it's her dream.
  - Effect: 💰-3, 🔧+1, 💭+2
- **B "先打工攒钱，稳一点"** → Get a safe job first (kitchen hand at another restaurant), save up.
  - Effect: 💰+1, 🔧+0, 💭-1 (patience tested)
- **C "和Minh商量"** → Ask Minh's opinion. He's hesitant — "We can't afford to gamble."
  - Effect: 💭-1, marriage_tension+1 (if she opens anyway), or 💰+0 (if she defers)

**Image note**: Small empty shopfront with "FOR LEASE" sign / Linh standing inside imagining the restaurant / cramped apartment kitchen.

---

### Ch2: 开张 (Opening Day)

**Setting**: "Pho Linh" restaurant, Marrickville (or the kitchen she works in if she chose B)

**Narrative**: Whether she opened right away or saved up first, the restaurant eventually opens. The first weeks are chaos — Australian food safety inspections, supplier negotiations in broken English, a health inspector who doesn't smile. But the pho is authentic and the locals start coming.

**Choices**:
- **A "什么都自己来，省钱"** → Do everything herself: cook, serve, clean, do the books. 16-hour days.
  - Effect: 💰+1, 🔧+2, 💭-2 (burnout)
- **B "请帮手，投入品质"** → Hire a local part-timer, invest in quality ingredients and a nice setup.
  - Effect: 💰-2, 🔧+1, 💭+1
- **C "和越南社区合作"** → Partner with another Vietnamese family — they bring resources, she brings the recipe and labor.
  - Effect: 💰+0, 🔧+1, 💭+1 (shared burden)

**Image note**: Steaming pho bowls / busy restaurant opening night / Linh exhausted but smiling / health inspection clipboard.

---

### Ch3: 裂痕 (Cracks)

**Setting**: 8 months into the restaurant. Home and restaurant.

**Narrative**: The restaurant is gaining regulars. A food blogger gave it 4 stars. But at home, things are fraying. Minh is deep in his PhD — stressed, absent, occasionally resentful of the restaurant consuming her time ("你开那个店到底为了什么？"). Mei asks why dad and mom don't eat dinner together anymore. Linh is caught between the thing she's building and the person her visa depends on.

**Choices**:
- **A "和Minh摊牌"** → Confront Minh. "I need you to see what I'm doing matters."
  - Effect: 💭+1 or -1 (depends on accumulated tension), marriage_tension resolves or escalates
- **B "专心经营，不管他了"** → Pour energy into the restaurant. Let the marriage drift.
  - Effect: 🔧+2, 💭-1, marriage_tension+2
- **C "周日关店，留给家庭"** → Close early every Sunday. Cook at home. Be present.
  - Requirement: 💭 >= 5
  - Effect: 💰-1, 💭+1, marriage_tension-1

**Image note**: Linh and Minh arguing in the kitchen, Mei doing homework in the background / Empty dinner table / Sunday family meal.

**Choices**:

  - Requirement: 🔧 >= 6
  - Effect: 💰-1, 🛂+1, 💭+1 (independence)

## Character 4: 阿杰 (Kevin) — NZ Citizen · 粤/闽南洋后裔

**Background**: 23 years old. His family's story starts long before him: 阿杰's祖辈是广东/福建下南洋的那一批人 — the generation that left home with nothing and scattered across Southeast Asia, chasing survival in the most turbulent decades of the 20th century. Grandpa ended up in New Zealand after a chain of detours through Malaysia and Singapore, working in a laundry, then a restaurant, then eventually owning a small import shop in Auckland. By the time 阿杰's parents were grown, the family had modest stability. His parents migrated to Sydney in the 2000s but he was born in New Zealand — making him a **NZ citizen by birth**.

He grew up split between Sydney and Guangzhou (sent back for a few years of Chinese schooling). His English is native-ish (slight Kiwi-Aussie hybrid accent), his Cantonese is fluent, his Mandarin is passable. As a NZ citizen he has automatic right to live and work in Australia under the Special Category Visa (subclass 444) — no PR needed, no visa anxiety, ever.

He never suffered. He was never rich either. This produced a very specific personality: **平和但散漫** — easygoing, kind, agreeable, and profoundly unmotivated. He enrolled at a Group of Eight university (family arranged it, paid the domestic fees since he qualifies), studies something vaguely practical like Information Systems, and treats uni as a social activity. His days: sleep till 10, maybe attend a lecture, find someone to have yum cha with, shoot some hoops in the afternoon, game until 2am. Repeat. Friends invite him camping or road-tripping; he says "下次吧" every time. There is no next time.

He has a loose network of relatives and family friends across Sydney — the kind of diaspora connections that come with being part of a Cantonese/Fujianese community that's been here for decades. The problem: most of them operate in grey areas. 表哥 runs a used car dealership in Bankstown that lowballs recent immigrants and hides mechanical defects. 叔公's friend is a migration agent who charges $10k for "guaranteed" visa advice. Another cousin does cash-only renovation work with no insurance. The cleanest person in the circle is 华叔 (Uncle Hua) — an old friend of grandpa's — who runs a legitimate Cantonese restaurant in Hurstville and actually pays his staff properly.

**Starting stats**: 💰5 🛂9 🔧2 💭4

**Core arc**: Three generations of hardship bought him total freedom — and he does nothing with it. The question: can a person with no external barriers find an internal reason to move?

---

### Ch1: 祖辈的余荫 (Grandfather's Shadow)

**Setting**: Sunday yum cha at 华叔's restaurant, Hurstville

**Narrative**: Sunday morning. 阿杰 is at the big round table at 华叔's restaurant — a weekly family-adjacent gathering. 华叔, 表哥, a couple of uncles, and assorted relatives are passing dishes and talking business. 表哥 is bragging about flipping a Camry for $3k profit: "那个印度哥们根本不懂车，我跟他说这车刚做完保养，他信了。" Everyone laughs. 华叔 doesn't laugh; he just pours tea.

阿杰 sits there eating char siu bao, half-listening. On the wall behind 华叔 is a faded photo: grandpa's generation, six young men outside a shop in Auckland, 1970s. They look thin and serious. 华叔 catches 阿杰 looking at it and says quietly: "你爷爷那辈人，从汕头坐船到南洋，死了一半。活下来的，什么都肯干。到你这一代，什么都不用干了。" It's not an accusation. It's an observation. It sits in 阿杰's chest like a stone.

After lunch, he goes home. Turns on the PC. Plays three rounds of Valorant. The stone is still there.

**Choices**:
- **A "华叔说得对，但跟我有什么关系"** → Shrug it off. Grandpa's story is grandpa's story. He plays another round. The feeling fades.
  - Effect: 💭+0, 🔧+0 (status quo maintained)
- **B "那张老照片让他想了很久"** → Something sticks. That night, instead of gaming, he sits on the balcony and thinks. About grandpa, about the boat, about what it means to have everything and want nothing.
  - Effect: 💭+1 (a crack in the inertia)
- **C "表哥倒是活得明白，跟他学学？"** → 表哥 makes money, has a nice car, doesn't overthink. Maybe that's the move. Text 表哥: "哥，你那车行还缺人不？"
  - Effect: 💰+1, 💭+0, 🔧+0 (easy path, grey zone beckons)

**Image note**: Yum cha round table, steam rising / faded 1970s photo on restaurant wall / 阿杰 alone on balcony at night, city lights below / 表哥 polishing a car at the dealership.

---

### Ch2: 混日子 (Drifting)

**Setting**: University campus → various Sydney locations

**Narrative**: Semester continues. 阿杰 goes to maybe 60% of his lectures. He's not failing — he's smart enough to pass without trying, which is exactly the problem. No urgency, no consequence, no friction. His days blur together: yum cha, basketball at the uni courts, a tutorial he half-prepared for, dinner at some cheap place in Burwood, gaming until the numbers on the clock stop meaning anything.

His phone buzzes constantly — but it's the grey-zone network. 表哥 wants help photographing cars for Gumtree listings ("就拍个照，很简单"). The migration agent uncle asks if any of his uni friends need "签证咨询." A cousin asks him to be a witness for a dodgy insurance claim. None of it is outright criminal, but none of it sits right either.

Meanwhile, 华叔's restaurant is struggling. Rent went up, a few staff quit, and 华叔 is too proud to cut corners. He mentions it over yum cha, casually, like it doesn't bother him. But 阿杰 notices the photo on the wall has been reframed. Someone cares about keeping it up.

**Choices**:
- **A "帮表哥拍拍车，赚点零花钱"** → Start helping at the car dealership. It's easy money and 表哥 is generous. But 阿杰 sees how the customers get treated — the newly-arrived immigrants who don't know market prices, the dodgy safety certificates. He's not doing the scamming, but he's in the room.
  - Effect: 💰+2, 💭-1 (complicity), 🔧+0
- **B "去华叔店里帮帮忙"** → Offer to help 华叔 at the restaurant. No pay expected — just wash dishes, bus tables, whatever. 华叔 doesn't say thank you. He just puts 阿杰 to work and starts teaching him how the kitchen runs.
  - Effect: 💰+0, 🔧+2 (learns real skills: cooking, management, dealing with suppliers), 💭+1 (purpose, however small)
- **C "都不想沾，自己待着"** → Neither grey zone nor responsibility. Stay home, game, coast through uni. It's safe, comfortable, and absolutely nothing is happening.
  - Effect: 💰+0, 🔧-1, 💭-1 (entropy)

**Image note**: 阿杰 holding a camera at a used car lot / 华叔's kitchen, busy dinner service, 阿杰 awkwardly carrying plates / 阿杰's room, curtains drawn at 2pm, monitor glow.

### Ch3: 灰与白 (Grey and White)

**Setting**: 6 months later. The grey-zone connections and the clean path collide.

**Narrative**: Something forces 阿杰's hand. The grey network isn't just background noise anymore — it's pulling him in, or pushing him away.

If he helped 表哥: the dealership is facing trouble. A customer — a WHV girl who bought a car that broke down on the highway — posted a complaint that went viral on Xiaohongshu. Fair Trading is investigating. 表哥 calls: "兄弟，帮我把那几条聊天记录删了" — asking 阿杰 to destroy evidence. This is no longer "just taking photos."

If he helped 华叔: the restaurant has slowly improved. 阿杰 actually enjoys the kitchen — there's something meditative about prep work, something satisfying about a dinner rush that runs smoothly. 华叔 starts talking about maybe expanding, or at least not closing. But he needs help with the business side — social media, online ordering — stuff he's too old-school to figure out.

If he stayed home: he's sliding. A uni assessment he genuinely forgot about gets him a formal academic warning. His housemate moves out. The room gets quieter.

**Choices**:
- **A "帮表哥删记录"** → Cross the line. Help cover up the dodgy business. It's family, right? That's what family does.
  - Effect: 💰+1, 💭-3 (he knows this is wrong), 🔧+0
  - Risk flag: this comes back in Ch4
- **B "拒绝表哥，和他划清界限"** → Say no. 表哥 gets angry: "你以为你多干净？你吃我的、用我的，现在装什么？" The family network fractures.
  - Effect: 💰-1, 💭+2 (integrity), 🔧+0
- **C "帮华叔把餐厅搞上去"** → Commit to the restaurant. Set up social media, register on Uber Eats, redesign the menu layout. Use the skills he's been wasting in gaming and uni to build something real.
  - Requirement: 🔧 >= 3 (need minimum capability)
  - Effect: 🔧+2, 💭+2, 💰+0
- **D "管他们的，我回国躺平"** → Enough of all this. He has NZ citizenship and enough Chinese to get by. Go back to Guangzhou, rent a cheap apartment, game all day, live on savings and family money. No one can tell him what to do.
  - Effect: 💭+1 (short-term relief), 🔧-1, 🛂 irrelevant (he can go anywhere)

**Image note**: 表哥 handing him a phone, "delete this" / 阿杰 at a laptop in 华叔's restaurant, setting up an online ordering page / 阿杰 packing a suitcase, looking at a one-way ticket to Guangzhou / 阿杰 sitting in the dark, academic warning letter on the desk.

### Ch4: 何去何从 (Which Way)

**Setting**: End of the academic year. University limping to a finish. The big question can't be deferred anymore.

**Narrative**: 阿杰 sits in his room. On the desk: his NZ passport (navy blue, silver fern), his Australian student ID, and his phone open to a real estate listing in Guangzhou — a one-bedroom apartment, 2000 RMB/month, near a gaming café. Also on the phone: a message from 华叔: "这个月营业额比去年同期涨了40%，你做的那些有用。"

He could apply for Australian citizenship — as a NZ citizen with enough residency, it's basically guaranteed. Then he'd have two passports: NZ and Australian. Total freedom. He could go anywhere, do anything, or — as the loudest voice in his head suggests — do nothing, anywhere.

The question his grandfather never had the luxury of asking: **when you can go anywhere, where do you actually want to be?**

**Choices**:
- **A "留下来，把华叔的餐厅做成自己的事业"** → He found something he cares about in the most unlikely place — grandpa's old friend's kitchen. Stay. Build. Maybe one day it's his restaurant, with that old photo still on the wall.
  - Requirement: 🔧 >= 5 AND 💭 >= 4
  - Effect: 🔧+2, 💭+2, 💰+1
- **B "拿了澳洲国籍，回国躺平"** → Get the citizenship, tick the box, fly to Guangzhou. Rent that cheap apartment. Game. Eat. Sleep. No stress, no expectations, no ambition. Grandpa crossed an ocean for this freedom — is lying flat the ultimate form of it, or the ultimate waste?
  - Effect: 💭+0, 🔧+0 (the flat line continues)
- **C "跟着灰色圈子继续混"** → 表哥's business recovered (or found new schemes). The money is easy. The company is familiar. The ethics are... flexible.
  - Requirement: chose A in Ch2 OR A in Ch3 (already in the grey zone)
  - Effect: 💰+3, 💭-2, 🔧+0
- **D "不知道，真的不知道"** → He can't choose. He's not broken — he's just... not ignited. He renews his enrollment for another semester. Another year. Another loop.
  - Effect: 💭-1, 🔧+0

**Image note**: NZ passport and AU student ID on desk / 华叔's restaurant with "NEW: Online Ordering!" sign / Guangzhou apartment listing on phone screen / 表哥's car lot at night, cash changing hands / lecture hall, empty seat.

### Ch5: Endings

Based on final stats after Ch4 — **three endings**:

**Ending A — "立业" (Building Something)**
- Condition: 🔧 >= 6 AND 💭 >= 5
- Story: 阿杰 applies for his Australian citizenship. It comes through in three months — anticlimactic, really, just a ceremony at Town Hall and a certificate. He doesn't feel different. But he stays.

  华叔's restaurant gets a renovation. Nothing fancy — new paint, better lighting, a proper logo that 阿杰 designed himself. They add a lunch special and a weekend brunch menu. The Xiaohongshu account 阿杰 runs hits 5000 followers. A food blogger comes and gives it a write-up. Friday nights are fully booked.

  华叔 hangs a second photo on the wall, next to grandpa's: 阿杰 and 华叔 in front of the restaurant on renovation day, paint on their shirts, grinning. Three generations of the same story, told in different kitchens.

  At yum cha, when 表哥 asks "你那个小饭馆能赚几个钱？", 阿杰 just smiles and pours the tea. He learned that from 华叔.

**Ending B — "躺平" (Lying Flat)**
- Condition: 💭 <= 3 AND did not commit to restaurant
- Story: Guangzhou. A one-bedroom apartment on the 12th floor. The curtains are drawn. It's 2pm. 阿杰 is on his third game of the day. The apartment costs 2000 RMB a month. His savings — plus the occasional red envelope from family — make the math work indefinitely.

  He has an NZ passport in the drawer and an Australian citizenship certificate in an unopened envelope. He could go anywhere in the world. He goes to the gaming café downstairs.

  His grandpa crossed an ocean with nothing. 阿杰 crossed an ocean with everything and went back. Is this freedom? He doesn't think about it. That's the point — he doesn't think about anything. The screen glows. The hours pass. Outside, Guangzhou hums with 14 million people who have somewhere to be.

  It's not tragic. It's just very, very quiet.

**Ending C — "灰幕" (The Grey Curtain)**
- Condition: 💰 >= 7 AND 💭 <= 3 (grey-zone path)
- Story: 表哥's business expanded. 阿杰 is in deeper than he meant to be — started with photos, then paperwork, then being the "face" that reassures nervous customers. He has money now. A nicer apartment. 表哥 bought him a car ("公司的，你用"). He drives past 华叔's restaurant sometimes and doesn't stop.

  At yum cha — they still go, every Sunday — 华叔 looks at him with the same expression he uses for 表哥. Not angry. Not disappointed. Just... old. Like he's seen this movie before, in an earlier generation, in a different country.

  One night, 阿杰 comes home and sees the NZ passport on the desk. He opens it to the photo page. The 16-year-old kid in the photo looks back at him. He looks like someone who hasn't decided who to be yet. 阿杰 closes the passport and puts it in the drawer.

  He's 24 and he has money and he has done nothing he's proud of.

## Design Notes

### Stat Flow & Balance
- Each character starts with 4 stats summing to roughly 12-15
- Each chapter offers choices that typically adjust 2-3 stats by +1 to +3 or -1 to -3
- After 4 chapters of choices, final stats determine ending
- Gated choices (requirements) reward earlier investment — creating a "your past choices shape your future options" feel

### Ending Logic
- Each character has 4 endings (A/B/C/D)
- Evaluated in order: check A condition first, then B, then C, then D (default/fallback)
- Makes total of 16 possible endings across the game

### Image Requirements
- 4 character portraits (for character select screen)
- ~5 scene images per character = ~20 scene images
- 4 × 4 = 16 ending images (can reuse/modify)
- Total: ~40 images (can reduce by reusing backgrounds with different overlays)

### Shared Sydney Elements
- Locations that appear across multiple characters: Ashfield, Hurstville, Marrickville, USYD, Sydney CBD, airport
- This creates a sense of shared world — the player realizes these characters could be passing each other on the street

### Music (Optional)
- If implemented: one ambient BGM per mood range (hopeful / tense / melancholy / triumphant)
- Graceful skip if audio module unavailable
